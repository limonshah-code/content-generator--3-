## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Floor Cleaner For Dog Pee

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
Before you purchase a cleaner, you must identify where the accidents are happening most frequently. If you have wall-to-wall carpeting, an enzymatic spray with a CRI Seal of Approval is essential to protect your fibers. For those with hardwood or tile, look specifically for hard-surface formulas that won't strip the sealant or leave a dull film. If you are dealing with a one-time accident, a 32-ounce spray is sufficient, but multi-pet households will save significantly by opting for gallon-sized refills.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($15-$60 ranges)
Prices for pet cleaners vary based on the concentration of active ingredients and the size of the container. A high-quality 32-ounce spray typically ranges from $15 to $25. Gallon sizes offer much better value, usually falling between $35 and $60. While generic cleaners might be cheaper, they often lack the enzymes needed to fully break down urine, which can lead to spending more money in the long run as you try to fix lingering odors.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
The most critical feature in a dog pee cleaner is the presence of enzymes or pro-bacteria. These biological components actually eat the organic matter that causes stains and smells.
